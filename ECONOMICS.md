# Economics

## What's a converted lead worth to Credex?

Credex sells discounted AI credits. Assume a typical deal:
- Average order value (AOV): **$2,000** (one quarter of discounted credits for a 10-person team)
- Gross margin on credits: **~30%** (reseller margin on overforecast inventory)
- Gross profit per deal: **$600**
- Annual repeat rate: assume **60%** of customers rebuy each quarter → LTV ≈ $600 × 2.4 quarters = **~$1,440 LTV per customer**

These are conservative estimates. The actual margin on liquidated credits could be higher depending on the source.

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by channel

| Channel | Estimated CAC | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Hacker News Show HN | ~$0 direct, 2h time | If post gets 100 audits, 5% book a call → 5 leads → $0/lead |
| Cold DM on X | ~$0 direct, 3h time | 20 DMs → 2 audits → 1 lead → "free" but time-boxed |
| Newsletter mention (sponsored) | ~$500/mention | 1,000 clicks → 200 audits → 10 leads → $50 CAC |
| Paid Twitter/X | ~$5–15 CPC | 100 clicks → 20 audits → 1 lead → $100–500 CAC |

**Conclusion:** Organic channels (HN, IH, Reddit) have near-zero CAC and positive unit economics at any conversion rate. Paid channels need >2% audit→consult conversion to break even.

## Conversion funnel math

```
100 visitors
  → 40 complete an audit (40% completion — form is short)
  → 8 submit email (20% of completers — value shown first)
  → 2 have >$500/mo savings (25% of email submitters)
  → 0.6 book a Credex consultation (30% of high-savings leads)
  → 0.3 purchase credits (50% close rate on consultations)

Revenue per 100 visitors: 0.3 × $2,000 = $600
Gross profit per 100 visitors: 0.3 × $600 = $180
```

At $0 CAC (organic), this is pure margin from the first user.

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 months

**Target:** $1M ARR = ~$83k/month in credit sales = ~42 deals/month at $2k AOV

**What has to be true:**

1. **Traffic:** 42 deals/month at 0.3% visitor→deal rate = **14,000 visitors/month** needed. Achievable via: SEO on "AI tool pricing" keywords (3–6 month lag), HN/IH referrals compounding, and 2–3 newsletter placements.

2. **Conversion rate holds:** 0.3% is conservative. If audit quality is high and the Credex pitch is tight, 0.5–1% is plausible → halves the traffic requirement.

3. **Deal size grows:** Teams auditing >$1k/mo spend will buy larger credit packages. AOV of $3–4k is realistic for 20+ person teams.

4. **Repeat purchases:** If 60% rebuy quarterly, you need fewer new customers each month. By month 12, repeat revenue covers ~40% of the target.

**Rough model:**

| Month | New customers | Repeat customers | MRR |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | 5/mo | 0 | $10k |
| 4–6 | 15/mo | 8/mo | $46k |
| 7–12 | 25/mo | 25/mo | $100k |
| 13–18 | 30/mo | 45/mo | $150k → $1.8M ARR |

This requires sustained traffic growth and a functioning sales motion by month 4. It's achievable but not guaranteed — the biggest risk is the consultation→purchase step, which depends on Credex's sales execution, not just the audit tool.
