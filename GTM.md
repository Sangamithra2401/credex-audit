# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact target user

**Engineering Manager at a 5–25 person Series A startup.** Specifically: someone who signs off on SaaS tool invoices, has 3–8 engineers on their team, and got their AI tooling budget approved 12–18 months ago during the hype peak. They're now being asked by the CFO to justify costs. They're not the CEO (too busy) and not a junior engineer (no budget authority). They're the exact person who Googles "is Cursor worth it for teams" or "GitHub Copilot vs Claude Code pricing."

Secondary user: **indie hacker / solo founder** paying for 2–3 AI tools personally, who has never done the math on what each costs per actual hour of use.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 budget

1. **Day 1–3: Post on Hacker News "Show HN"** — "Show HN: Free AI spend auditor for dev teams." Write a comment explaining the unit economics problem (most teams don't know their per-seat cost). HN loves tools that solve real procurement pain. Target: 300–500 visitors, 50–80 audits.

2. **Day 4–7: Post in Indie Hackers** — "I built a free tool to audit AI tool spend after realizing my team was wasting $400/month. Here's what I found." Link the tool with a real audit screenshot. Target: 30–50 signups.

3. **Day 5–10: DM 20 engineering managers on X** — Find people who've posted about "AI tools" or "developer productivity" in the last 30 days. Send a one-line cold DM: "Built a free audit tool for AI tool spend — curious if your number matches what I'd estimate for a team your size." No pitch, just curiosity trigger.

4. **Day 7–14: r/ExperiencedDevs + r/cscareerquestions post** — "How much does your team actually spend on AI tools? I audited ours and found $X/month wasted. Built a free checker." Genuine post, link in comments. These communities hate self-promotion but reward useful tools.

5. **Day 10–20: Reach out to 5 developer-focused newsletters** — TLDR Dev, Bytes.dev, JavaScript Weekly, Node Weekly. Offer a free audit for their own team in exchange for a mention.

6. **Day 15–30: Tweet the audit output** — Generate a fake (clearly labeled) audit for a hypothetical "$50k ARR startup" and share it as an example. The OG tags make the share URL look clean in Twitter feeds.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that overforecast AI credits. Those companies' customers are exactly the people who are overspending. Credex could offer the audit tool as a free onboarding step for every new lead — "before we sell you credits, let's show you exactly where you're wasting money." This makes the audit a natural part of the Credex sales motion, not just a cold acquisition tool.
